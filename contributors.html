Contribution Guide

Help thousands of students by sharing your knowledge. Learn how to contribute quality study materials.

Why Your Contribution Matters

Every note, practical manual, or resource you share helps students across the country. Your work gets properly credited, and you become part of a community building the future of accessible education.

What You Can Contribute

📝

Study Notes

Unit-wise notes covering syllabus topics with diagrams and explanations.

  • Well-structured and organized
  • Aligned with official syllabus
  • Includes diagrams where needed
  • PDF format preferred
🔬

Practical Manuals

Step-by-step practical experiments with observations and conclusions.

  • Clear procedure steps
  • Circuit diagrams included
  • Expected observations
  • Safety precautions
📄

Previous Year Questions

Question papers from past examinations to help students prepare.

  • Clear scanned copies
  • Year and exam type mentioned
  • All questions visible
  • Solutions (if available)
🎥

Video Tutorials

Educational video links that explain concepts clearly.

  • High-quality content
  • Clear audio and visuals
  • Covers specific topics
  • YouTube links preferred

How to Contribute

1

Prepare Your Content

Organize your notes, practicals, or resources. Ensure they're clear, accurate, and follow our quality guidelines listed below.

2

Format Requirements

For Notes & Practicals: PDF format, max 10MB per file
For PYQs: Clear scans, PDF format
For Videos: YouTube links with topic details

3

Submit Your Work

Email your content to diplomanotes.web.com with the subject line: "Content Contribution - [Branch] - [Subject]"

4

Review Process

Our team reviews submissions with faculty guidance. You'll receive feedback within 3-5 days. Approved content gets published with your credit.

5

Get Credited

Once approved, your name appears on our contributors page with links to your social profiles (optional).

Quality Guidelines

✓

Do's

  • Use clear, readable fonts (11pt or larger)
  • Include diagrams and illustrations
  • Verify accuracy of information
  • Follow syllabus structure
  • Provide proper citations
  • Use professional language
✗

Don'ts

  • Don't copy directly from textbooks
  • Don't include personal opinions
  • Don't submit blurry scans
  • Don't use copyrighted images
  • Don't include incorrect information
  • Don't submit incomplete content
